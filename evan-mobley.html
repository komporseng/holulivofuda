Evan Mobley- Biography

Evan Mobley is a professional basketball player for the Cleveland Cavaliers of the National Basketball Association. He previously played for the USC Trojans in college. The Cleveland Cavaliers selected him third overall in the NBA Draft 2021.

Evan Mobley- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Evan Mobley was born on June 18, 2001. He was raised alongside his older brother, Isaiah. Evan Mobley and his older brother Isaiah started playing basketball at a young age with the help of their father, who was also a former basketball player. Evan was first hesitant to play basketball, but he became interested in the eighth school. He was nearly 6’4″ tall in eighth grade. Later, as a student at Rancho Christian School in California, he began playing school basketball. He was a teammate of his older brother Isaiah, a five-star recruit in the 2019 class, for the first three years of his career.

As a junior, he averaged roughly 19.2 points, 10.4 rebounds, and 4.7 blocks per game while playing basketball at his California high school. He was named California Gatorade Player of the Year as well as The Press-Enterprise Player of the Year. Evan led his basketball career as a senior, averaging 20.5 points, 12.2 rebounds, 5.2 blocks, and 4.6 assists per game while playing for Rancho Christian School. He finished with a 22-8 overall record.

Evan Mobley- Professional Career

Mobley was regarded as a five-star prospect and was ranked as one of the top three players in the 2020 recruiting class. On August 5, 2019, he committed to play basketball for USC, turning down offers from UCLA, Washington, and other prominent NCAA Division I programs. Later, he rose to become one of the program’s top players.

Mobley made his USC debut on November 25, 2020, scoring 21 points and grabbing 9 rebounds in a 95-87 victory over California Baptist. On March 11, 2021, in the quarterfinals of the Pac-12 Tournament, he scored a career-high 26 points, 9 rebounds, and 5 blocks in a 91-85 win over Utah. In a 72-70 semifinals defeat against Colorado, he scored 26 points for the second time, grabbed 9 rebounds, and blocked 5 shots.

NBA

The Cleveland Cavaliers selected Mobley third overall in the 2021 NBA Draft. On August 3rd, he formalized his deal with the Cavs, and on August 8th, 2021, he made his summer league debut with an 84-76 loss against the Houston Rockets, in which he scored 12 points, 5 rebounds, and 3 blocks in 28 minutes. On October 20, 2021, he made his NBA debut, scoring 17 points, 9 rebounds, and 6 assists in a 132-121 loss to the Memphis Grizzlies.

Trivia

  • Mobley represented the United States at the 2018 FIBA Under-17 World Cup. He averaged 9.3 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 2.6 assists per game over the course of seven games, leading his team to a gold medal.
  • Mobley received several awards, including Morgan Wootten National Player of the Year.
  • He was also chosen to play in a few events, including the Jordan Brand Classic, McDonald’s All-American Game, and Nike Hoop Summit, but all three were canceled due to the pandemic COVID-19.
  • Mobley earned Pac-12 Player of the Year, Defensive Player of the Year, and Freshman of the Year honors.
  • He had the right to be the second player from the major conference to win all three accolades.
  • On April 16, 2021, it was revealed that he would declare for the 2021 NBA draft prior to the end of his NCAA eligibility.
