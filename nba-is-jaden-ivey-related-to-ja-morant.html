Let’s find out “NBA: Is Jaden Ivey Related To Ja Morant?” Although both NBA basketball players Jaden Ivey and Ja Morant are black, they are unrelated to one another.

The Detroit Pistons selected Purdue basketball player Ivey with the fifth overall pick in the 2022 NBA Draft. The 20-year-old spent his youth competing in sports including football, basketball, soccer, and karate.

He started to put more emphasis on basketball as a high school student. Ivey attended Marian High School in Mishawaka, Indiana for the first three years of his playing career.

For his senior year, he switched schools and enrolled in one of the top programs in the country at La Lumiere School in La Porte, Indiana. A four-star recruit overall, he turned down invitations to play college basketball from Butler and Notre Dame in favor of Purdue.

Jaden Ivey and Ja Morant are not blood brothers or related in any other way. They might, however, be bonded by the fact that they are both NBA basketball players.

The young athlete has only one sibling, a half-brother named Jordan Hunter from his father.

Insight on Jaden Ivey’s Parents And Family Tree

Jaden Ivey was born to parents Niele Ivey and Javin Hunter one day before Valentine’s Day in Indiana on February 13, 2002. His family tree boasts athletes going all the way back to his grandfather.

His mother is Notre Dame’s head women’s basketball coach. In addition to being an All-American player at Notre Dame, she spent five seasons playing in the WNBA.

On the other hand, his father is a former football wide receiver who was selected by the Baltimore Ravens in the sixth round of the 2002 NFL Draft after playing collegiate football for the University of Notre Dame.

Furthermore, Javin Hunter spent a season playing with the San Francisco 49ers. Jaden’s grandfather, James Hunter, was also a former football player who participated in NFL football with the Detroit Lions.
