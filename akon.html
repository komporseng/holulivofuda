A Professional Life of Akon

Akon began singing and performing as a teenager after growing up in Jersey City, New Jersey. Instead of attending university, he chose to compose music with his friend, Wyclef Jean of the Fugees, using home recordings. In 2003, he signed his first recording contract, and his debut album was published in 2004.

Trouble is the title of the album. The album was a big smash, combining Akon’s melodic R&B vocals with hip hop beats and yielding multiple hit singles, including “Locked Up” and “Lonely.” In 2006, he released his second album, Konvicted, which was even more successful. With collaborations with musicians like Eminem and Snoop Dogg, several singles made the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Freedom, his third album, was released in 2008, although it did not have the same level of success as his first two albums. He has collaborated with some of the biggest names in music during the course of his career. For example, he collaborated on the song “Hold my hand” with Michael Jackson and co-wrote “Just Dance” with Lady Gaga.

Apart from music, Akon owns two clothing lines, Konvict and Aliaune, as well as a substantial real estate holdings. He is also the creator of the Konfidence Foundation, which aims to empower underprivileged youth in Africa and the United States.
